Tagalong Complete Guide

Everything you need to know — from first install to advanced workflows.

Getting Started

Get Tagalong up and running on your Mac in just a few minutes. This section walks you through system requirements, installation, licensing setup, and your very first recording.

System Requirements

Tagalong is a native macOS application built with SwiftUI. It ships as a universal binary, so it runs natively on both Apple Silicon (M1, M2, M3, M4) and Intel-based Macs.

Requirement Details
macOS 15.0 (Sequoia) or later
Architecture Apple Silicon or Intel (universal binary)
Microphone Built-in or external mic (required)
Internet Required for transcription and AI processing
Disk space ~25 MB for the app; recordings stored in ~/Documents/Tagalong/
Conference Mode (system audio capture) uses ScreenCaptureKit, which requires macOS 14 or later and Screen Recording permission. Standard mic-only recording does not need this permission.

Installation

Tagalong is distributed as a notarized DMG disk image. There is no installer to run — just drag and drop.

  1. Download Tagalong — Visit tagalongai.com and click the Download button. Your browser will download Tagalong.dmg.
  2. Open the DMG — Double-click the downloaded file. A Finder window will appear showing the Tagalong app icon and an arrow pointing to your Applications folder.
  3. Drag to Applications — Drag Tagalong.app onto the Applications folder alias. This copies the app to your Mac.
  4. Launch Tagalong — Open Tagalong from your Applications folder (or use Spotlight: press ⌘ + Space, type "Tagalong", and press Enter). You will see a microphone icon appear in your menu bar.
  5. Grant Microphone permission — On first launch, macOS will ask for Microphone access. Click Allow — this is required for recording to work. You can also grant this later in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.
[SCREENSHOT: getting-started-permissions]
Tip: Tagalong runs as a menu bar app — it does not appear in the Dock. Click the microphone icon in your menu bar to open the popover window.
Screen Recording permission is only needed if you plan to use Conference Mode (which captures system audio from apps like Zoom or Google Meet). You can skip this for now and grant it later when you first enable Conference Mode.

Setup & API Keys

When you first open Tagalong, you will see the onboarding screen. Tagalong offers two ways to unlock Pro features — choose whichever fits your workflow best.

[SCREENSHOT: getting-started-onboarding]

Subscription — $9.99/mo

The simplest way to get started. Tagalong manages all API access through its own backend — you do not need to create accounts with OpenAI, AssemblyAI, or Google. Just subscribe, and everything works immediately.

Private License — $199 one-time

Bring your own API keys. You sign up for OpenAI, AssemblyAI, and optionally Google Gemini, then enter your keys in Settings. Your audio and transcripts go directly to those providers — they never touch Tagalong servers.

You do not have to choose right away. Tagalong works on the Free tier without any setup at all — you get 5 meetings per month with mic recording, speaker identification, a basic AI summary, and markdown export. No credit card required.

Setting up a Subscription

  1. Open Settings — Click the gear icon in the Tagalong popover.
  2. Select Subscription mode — Under Licensing Mode, choose "Subscription."
  3. Complete checkout — Tagalong will open a Stripe checkout page in your browser. After payment, the app activates automatically.
[SCREENSHOT: getting-started-settings-subscription]

Setting up a Private License

  1. Get your API keys — You will need an OpenAI API key (for Whisper transcription and GPT summaries) and an AssemblyAI API key (for speaker diarization). A Google Gemini API key is optional and only needed for infographic generation.
  2. Open Settings — Click the gear icon in the Tagalong popover.
  3. Select Private License mode — Under Licensing Mode, choose "Private License."
  4. Enter your keys — Paste each API key into the corresponding field. Keys are stored securely in macOS Keychain and are never sent to Tagalong servers.
[SCREENSHOT: getting-started-settings-private]
Tip: You can switch between Subscription and Private License at any time in Settings. Both modes unlock the full set of Pro features.

Your First Recording

Once Tagalong is installed and you have chosen a licensing mode (or are using the Free tier), you are ready to record your first meeting.

  1. Click the menu bar icon — Click the microphone icon in your Mac's menu bar to open Tagalong's popover.
  2. Name your meeting — Type a name in the meeting name field (e.g., "Weekly Standup"). This is optional — Tagalong will use a timestamp if you leave it blank. If you have Calendar integration enabled, it may auto-fill the name of your current or upcoming meeting.
  3. Click Start Recording — Tagalong begins listening through your selected microphone. You will see a pulsing red dot and a live waveform. Speak normally — the app transcribes in the background every 10 seconds.
[SCREENSHOT: getting-started-idle]

When your meeting ends, click Stop Recording. Tagalong will process the audio — uploading it for speaker diarization, identifying who said what, and generating an AI summary. This typically takes 30 seconds to a couple of minutes depending on meeting length.

Your results are saved automatically to ~/Documents/Tagalong/{meeting-name}/ and you can review the transcript, summary, infographic, and notes right inside the app.

Tip: You can also start and stop recording from anywhere on your Mac using the global shortcut ⌘ + ⇧ + R — no need to click the menu bar icon first.

Recording a Meeting

Tagalong captures your meetings right from the macOS menu bar. Choose between microphone-only mode for in-person meetings or conference mode for virtual calls — then let Tagalong handle the rest.

Starting a Recording

Click the microphone icon in your macOS menu bar to open Tagalong's popover. From the idle screen you can name your meeting, choose an audio mode, and start recording in seconds.

  1. Open Tagalong — Click the mic icon in the menu bar. A 420×580 popover appears.
  2. Name your meeting — Type a meeting name or leave it blank (Tagalong will use a timestamp). If you have a Pro license and have granted calendar access, the name auto-fills from your next calendar event.
  3. Choose your audio mode — Select Microphone (default) or Conference Mode PRO using the mode toggle.
  4. Click Start Recording — Tagalong begins capturing audio immediately. The menu bar icon turns into a red recording indicator.
[SCREENSHOT: recording-start-screen]
Tip: You can also press ⌘ + ⇧ + R from anywhere on your Mac to start or stop a recording — no need to open the popover first.

Microphone Mode vs Conference Mode

Tagalong offers two audio capture modes depending on where your meeting audio is coming from.

Microphone Mode

Captures only your microphone input. Best for in-person meetings, phone calls, or any situation where the audio you want to record comes through your mic. No additional permissions beyond Microphone access are required.

Conference Mode PRO

Captures both your microphone and system audio simultaneously — perfect for Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and other virtual meeting apps. No browser extension, no bot, and no meeting URL needed.

Conference Mode requirements: macOS 14 or later, plus Screen Recording permission (System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording). Grant the permission to Tagalong and restart the app if prompted. Video overhead is negligible — Tagalong captures a 2×2 pixel stream at 1 fps solely to activate the audio pipeline.
System audio not being captured? Verify that Screen Recording permission is granted in System Settings, then quit and reopen Tagalong. Make sure you selected "Conference Mode" before clicking Start Recording.

During Recording

While recording, Tagalong shows a live interface with real-time feedback about the audio it is capturing. The recording view is laid out from top to bottom as follows:

  1. Status bar — A pulsing red dot, "Recording" label, and elapsed timer at the top.
  2. Audio waveform — An animated visualization of your current audio levels.
  3. "Last Heard" card — Shows the latest transcribed chunk from Whisper, updated every ~10 seconds. Displays "Listening..." when waiting for speech, or "Silence detected" when no audio is present.
  4. Word count stats — A row showing how many words have been captured so far.
  5. Meeting name — An editable text field. You can rename the meeting at any time during recording.
  6. Microphone selector — A dropdown to switch input devices mid-recording.
  7. Meeting Notes area — A text input for capturing timestamped notes (see below).
  8. Stop Recording — Saves the audio and starts processing.
  9. Cancel — Discards the recording entirely and returns to the idle screen.
[SCREENSHOT: recording-active]
Tip: The "Last Heard" preview is cosmetic — it shows quick Whisper chunks for your awareness. The final transcript comes from AssemblyAI's speaker diarization pipeline after you stop recording, which is far more accurate.

Recording Start Sound

Tagalong can play an audible notification tone shortly after recording begins — a courtesy alert so meeting participants know the call is being recorded.

  1. Enable the setting — Open Settings and toggle Recording Start Sound under the General section.
  2. Start a recording — Two seconds after recording begins, a brief notification tone plays through your speakers.
  3. Automatic guard — If you cancel recording within those two seconds, no sound plays.
Note: This setting is off by default. The sound plays through your system output, so meeting participants on a call will hear it.

Taking Meeting Notes

Capture important moments as they happen. Notes you take during recording are automatically timestamped so you can connect them to specific points in the conversation.

  1. Type your note — Enter text in the notes area at the bottom of the recording view.
  2. Send it — Press ⌘ + Return or click the send button. The note is saved with a timestamp like "[0:04:32] Agreed on Q3 launch date".
  3. View your notes — Submitted notes appear in a collapsible list below the input. After recording, they show up in the Notes tab of your results.
[SCREENSHOT: recording-notes]

Notes are included in the AI summary prompt, so important context you capture is reflected in the final output. They are also saved to a plain-text file ({meeting-name}_notes.txt) that you can edit by hand at any time.

Live AI Chat PRO

Ask questions about your meeting while it is still happening. The "Ask AI" panel uses your accumulated live transcript as context and streams answers in real time.

  1. Expand the panel — Tap the collapsible "Ask AI" bar at the bottom of the recording screen.
  2. Ask a question — Type something like "What was the main concern raised?" or "Summarize the last 5 minutes."
  3. Read the response — Answers stream in as they are generated. The chat does not interfere with or pause your recording.
[SCREENSHOT: recording-chat]
How it works: Live AI Chat sends the transcript accumulated so far to GPT and streams back a response. Your recording continues uninterrupted in the background — the chat runs on a separate thread.

Calendar Auto-fill PRO

  1. Grant permission — The first time you use this feature, Tagalong asks for Calendar access (System Settings → Privacy & Security → Calendars).
  2. See your next meeting — A card on the idle screen shows your next upcoming calendar event, refreshing every 30 seconds.
  3. Click to auto-fill — Tap the calendar card to populate the meeting name field instantly.

Switching Microphones Mid-Recording

Need to swap from your AirPods to a USB mic? Tagalong handles microphone changes on the fly without losing any audio data.

Use the microphone selector dropdown on the recording screen to pick a different input device. Tagalong disconnects from the current mic and reconnects to the new one seamlessly — no gap in your recording.

Bluetooth recovery: If a Bluetooth audio device disconnects unexpectedly, Tagalong retries the connection up to 5 times with increasing delays. If it still cannot reconnect, it automatically falls back to your Mac's built-in microphone so you never lose a recording.

Stopping & Cancelling

When your meeting is over, you have two choices:

Stop Recording

Saves the captured audio and moves to the processing stage. Tagalong runs speaker diarization via AssemblyAI, then generates your summary and infographic. You will see a progress screen while this happens.

Cancel

Discards the recording entirely — no audio is saved and no processing occurs. You return to the idle screen. Use this if you started a recording by accident.

Tip: You can also stop a recording with the global shortcut ⌘ + ⇧ + R. This behaves the same as clicking Stop Recording — it saves your audio and begins processing.

Your Results

After you stop recording (or import a file), Tagalong processes the audio and presents your meeting as an interactive, tab-based results view. Here you can review who said what, read an AI-generated summary, view an infographic, browse your notes, and ask follow-up questions about the discussion.

Processing Steps

When you stop a recording, Tagalong runs through a series of steps automatically. You will see each step displayed in sequence so you always know where things stand.

  1. Uploading audio — Your recorded WAV file is uploaded for processing.
  2. Transcribing — OpenAI Whisper converts the audio to text.
  3. Identifying speakers — AssemblyAI diarization figures out who said what, labeling each segment by speaker.
  4. Generating summary — GPT reads the full transcript and produces a structured summary with key decisions, action items, and takeaways.
  5. Done — Results are saved to your output folder and the results view opens.
Processing time depends on meeting length. A 30-minute meeting typically takes 1 to 3 minutes. The menu bar icon shows a spinning arrow while processing is in progress.

The Results View

Once processing completes, Tagalong opens the results view with five tabs. Each tab gives you a different perspective on the meeting.

Transcript Summary Infographic Notes Chat

Switch between tabs by clicking the pill at the top of the results view. The Transcript tab is shown by default.

Transcript Tab

The transcript tab shows every segment of the conversation, labeled by speaker. Each entry displays a colored speaker pill, a timestamp, and the spoken text.

[SCREENSHOT: results-transcript]

  • Timestamps mark the start of each segment

Tip: The transcript search bar is great for quickly finding when a specific topic was discussed. Type a keyword and only matching entries will be shown.

Speaker Editing

Diarization labels speakers generically as "Speaker A," "Speaker B," and so on. You can replace these with real names so the transcript, summary, and infographic all reflect who actually said what.

[SCREENSHOT: results-speaker-edit]
  1. Click the pencil icon — Next to any speaker name (e.g., "Speaker A"), click the edit icon.
  2. Type the real name — Enter the person's actual name in the text field.
  3. Save — The name change propagates immediately through the entire transcript, the AI summary, and the infographic.
Tip: Edit speaker names before generating the summary or infographic. The AI uses the names you provide, so "Alice" and "Bob" will produce much more readable output than "Speaker A" and "Speaker B."

Summary Tab

The summary tab displays a markdown-rendered AI summary generated by GPT. It is structured into clear sections — typically key decisions, action items, and key takeaways — so you can quickly grasp what happened without rereading the full transcript.

[SCREENSHOT: results-summary]

If you want a different format, choose a summary recipe before generating. You can also refine the summary after it has been created using AI editing.

Summary Recipes PRO

Recipes let you control the format and focus of the AI summary. Select a recipe before clicking Generate Summary — the recipe's system prompt replaces the default one, so the AI tailors the output accordingly.

[SCREENSHOT: results-recipes]

You can also create custom recipes with your own name and system prompt. Custom recipes are stored locally and appear alongside the built-in options in the recipe picker.

AI Summary Editing PRO

After a summary has been generated, you can refine it using natural language instructions. The edit panel appears below the summary content.

  1. Type an instruction — Describe what you want changed in plain English: "Make it shorter," "Add a risk section," "Remove the action items," or "Rewrite the intro for a technical audience."
  2. Review the proposal — The AI generates a refined version of the summary. You see it as a proposed edit.
  3. Accept or Reject — Click Accept to apply the change, or Reject to discard it and keep the original.
  4. Undo if needed — Click Undo to revert to the previous version. Tagalong keeps a history stack so you can step back through multiple edits.
Tip: The AI uses the full transcript as context when editing, not just the current summary text. This means you can ask it to add information that was discussed but not included in the original summary.

Exporting Your Results

Tagalong gives you several ways to share or archive your meeting results, depending on your workflow and tier.

Markdown Export

Always available, even on the Free tier. Saves the transcript with speaker labels and a Meeting Notes section to a .md file in your output folder.

PDF Export PRO

Generates a formatted PDF document. A save dialog lets you choose where to save the file on your Mac.

Slack PRO

Share the summary directly to a Slack channel. Paste your incoming webhook URL in Settings, then click "Share to Slack" in the results view. Sends a formatted Block Kit message.

Notion PRO

Export the meeting as a new page in a Notion database. Enter your Notion API key and Database ID in Settings, then click "Export to Notion."

Tip: Set up Slack and Notion integration once in Settings, and sharing becomes a single click after every meeting. See the Integrations section for setup details.

Managing Sessions

Tagalong saves every meeting locally on your Mac. Browse past sessions, search across all your meetings, import recordings from other tools, and revisit any previous session whenever you need it.

Session History PRO

The Session History view gives you a chronological list of every meeting Tagalong has processed. Each entry shows the meeting name, date, and duration so you can quickly find the session you need.

  1. Open Session History — Click the "Session History" button on the idle screen.
  2. Browse sessions — Sessions are sorted by date with the most recent at the top. Each row shows the meeting name and the date it was recorded.
  3. Open a session — Click any session to load it into the Results view, where you can review the transcript, summary, infographic, and notes just as you did when the meeting ended.
[SCREENSHOT: sessions-history]
Tip: Session History reads from your output folder at ~/Documents/Tagalong/. If you move or rename session folders in Finder, Session History will reflect those changes the next time you open it.

Cross-Meeting Search PRO

Need to find something a colleague said three weeks ago? Cross-Meeting Search lets you search keywords across every transcript and notes file in your Tagalong output folder.

  1. Open Search — Click the "Search" button on the idle screen.
  2. Enter a keyword — Type any word or phrase. Search is case-insensitive.
  3. Browse results — Each result shows the meeting name, the matched line with your keyword highlighted, the speaker who said it, and the date. Results are sorted newest first, with a maximum of 100 results.
  4. Open a result — Click any result to open that session in the Results view.
[SCREENSHOT: sessions-search]
Note: Search scans both .md transcript files and _notes.txt files, so your meeting notes are searchable too.

File Import PRO

  1. Click Import File — On the idle screen, click the "Import File" button.
  2. Select your file — Choose an audio file or a transcript file from your Mac.
  3. Wait for processing — Audio files are sent to AssemblyAI for speaker diarization (this may take a minute or two). Transcript files are parsed immediately.
  4. Review results — Once processing is complete, you will see the full Results view with transcript, summary options, and more.
Format Type Processing

vtt, srt Transcript Parsed (Zoom format)

Open Previous Session

Revisit any session you have already recorded or imported. This feature is available on all tiers, including Free.

  1. Click Open Previous Session — On the idle screen, click "Open Previous Session." A folder picker opens, starting in ~/Documents/Tagalong/.
  2. Select a session folder — Choose the folder for the meeting you want to reopen.
  3. View results — Tagalong loads the transcript, speaker names, summary, notes, and infographic back into the Results view.
How it works: If the folder contains a .md file, Tagalong parses it to restore the full session. If only an audio file is present (no markdown), it runs diarization to create the transcript from scratch.

Audio Playback PRO

If a session includes an audio file (from a live recording or an imported audio file), a player bar appears in the Results view so you can listen back.

  • Scrubable progress bar — drag to any point in the recording
  • Volume control slider
  • Click any transcript entry to seek directly to that moment in the audio
  • Time display showing current position and total duration
Tip: Clicking a transcript row jumps the audio to that speaker's start time. This is useful for verifying a specific quote or catching something the transcription may have missed.

Output Folder Structure

Every session is saved to a dedicated folder inside ~/Documents/Tagalong/. Here is what each folder contains.

File Description
{name}.md Full transcript with speaker labels, AI summary, and meeting notes
{name}_notes.txt Human-editable notes in [timestamp] text format
{name}_infographic.jpg 4K generated infographic (if created)
{name}.wav Raw audio recording (optional; may be large)
Note: These are standard files on your Mac. You can open the markdown in any text editor, share the infographic as an image, or back up the entire folder to cloud storage.

Licensing Modes

Tagalong offers two ways to unlock Pro features. Both grant the same full feature set — the difference is how API access is handled.

Subscription — $9.99/mo

Zero setup. Tagalong manages all API access through its own backend proxy at api.tagalongai.com. You do not need API keys from OpenAI, AssemblyAI, or Google. Just subscribe and start recording.

Private License — $199 one-time

Bring your own API keys for maximum privacy. Your audio and transcripts go directly to the providers — they never touch Tagalong servers. Better for organizations with strict data policies.

Free tier: Without either license, Tagalong works in Free mode — mic recording, speaker identification, basic AI summary, and markdown export for up to 5 meetings per month. No credit card required.

Subscription Setup

The Subscription option is the quickest way to unlock Pro. No API keys needed — Tagalong handles everything through its backend.

  1. Open Settings — Click the gear icon in the Tagalong popover.

Summary Model

By default, Tagalong uses GPT-4o for generating meeting summaries. You can change this in Settings if you prefer a different model for cost or speed reasons. The selected model is stored locally and applies to all future summaries.

Note: This setting only applies to Private License mode. Subscription users use the model configured on the Tagalong backend.

Custom Vocabulary

Improve transcription accuracy for company names, product names, people's names, and industry jargon by adding them to your custom vocabulary list.

  1. Open Settings — Click the gear icon.
  2. Find Custom Vocabulary — Scroll to the Custom Vocabulary section.
  3. Add your terms — Enter words separated by commas or newlines. For example: Tagalong, AssemblyAI, OpenAI, Q3 launch, COGS

These terms are sent to AssemblyAI as a word_boost parameter, which biases the speech recognition model toward recognizing these words correctly. "Tagalong" is included by default.

Tip: Add the names of meeting participants, your company's product names, and any acronyms or abbreviations that come up frequently. This significantly reduces transcription errors for domain-specific terms.

Integrations PRO

Share meeting results directly to Slack or Notion without leaving Tagalong.

Slack

Send meeting summaries to any Slack channel via an incoming webhook. The summary is formatted using Slack Block Kit for clean, readable output.

Notion

Export meetings as new pages in a Notion database. Each export creates a page with the transcript, summary, and notes organized into blocks.

Setting up Slack

  1. Create a webhook — Go to api.slack.com/apps, create or select an app, enable Incoming Webhooks, and add a new webhook to the channel you want.
  2. Copy the webhook URL — It looks like https://hooks.slack.com/services/T.../B.../...
  3. Paste in Settings — Open Tagalong Settings, find the Slack section, and paste the URL.
  4. Share — After generating a summary, click the Share to Slack button in the Results view.

Setting up Notion

  1. Create an integration — Go to notion.so/my-integrations and create a new internal integration. Copy the API key.
  2. Share your database — Open the Notion database where you want meeting pages created, click Share, and invite your integration.
  3. Get the database ID — Open the database in Notion. The database ID is the 32-character string in the URL between the workspace name and the question mark.
  4. Paste in Settings — Enter both the Notion API key and Database ID in Tagalong Settings.
  5. Export — After a meeting, click the Export to Notion button in the Results view.
